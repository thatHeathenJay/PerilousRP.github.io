Rules

Perilous Roleplay's owners and administration have established comprehensive rules governing general gameplay, gang activities, and business operations. These guidelines are designed to ensure a fair and enjoyable experience for all players. Please review these rules carefully, as your participation in the server implies agreement to abide by them.

General Roleplay Rules

Player Reports and Bans

All player reports must be submitted through our Discord server. To report an incident or individual, open a ticket in the '#open-ticket' channel with relevant information. Community members should never directly contact Administration or staff members. Hostile behavior towards Staff through direct messages or tickets may result in a ban, depending on severity. Staff will proactively enforce community rules and guidelines without the need for reports.

General Rules

  • Minimum age requirement: 18 years old
  • A functional, high-quality microphone is mandatory
  • Character names must be realistic
  • Players must adhere to all Twitch.tv Terms of Service (TOS) at all times
  • Toxic behavior or inciting toxicity on any platform (including Twitch, YouTube, Discord, Forums, etc.) may result in removal from the community
  • Zero tolerance for racism, homophobia, sexism, bigotry, or other offensive actions and/or language
  • Sexual Roleplay is strictly prohibited under all circumstances
  • Hacking, exploiting, anti-cheat detection, or any form of cheating will result in permanent removal
  • Referencing terms alluding to a second character (e.g., cousin, brother) during roleplay is not permitted
  • Suicide RP is strictly forbidden
  • Use of low-resolution texture packs, FOV mods, tracers, shooting effects, etc., is prohibited and may lead to permanent removal from the community

Character Rules

  • Maintain character at all times. In the event of poor roleplay or character breaking by others, follow the guidelines for player reports without breaking character yourself
  • Do not interrupt, provoke, or impose yourself into ongoing scenarios unless you have a legitimate association with the scene
  • Rejoining scenarios after they have concluded, including after being incapacitated, is not allowed. Once you're gone, you're considered 'at large'
  • "Cop baiting" (repeatedly attempting to gain police attention) is prohibited
  • Initiation is required for any form of combat. Be within shouting range and clearly state your intent. If there's no response after two attempts, proceed as necessary
  • Combat storing (intentionally disconnecting to store items before returning to a scene) is forbidden
  • Civilians are prohibited from wearing law enforcement, emergency services, or correctional officer clothing
  • Gunplay over Roleplay is never acceptable. Prioritize verbal interaction over weapon use
  • Giving away items or money due to a character going DOA or leaving the server is prohibited and will result in item/character wipes for all involved parties
  • Use of fake hostages or repeated use of the same hostage is not allowed
  • Avoid initiating major illegal activities within 30 minutes before or after server restarts
  • "Loot Boxing" (looting players with minimal roleplay) is strictly prohibited
  • Using out-of-game information to influence in-game roleplay is not permitted
  • Unprovoked attacks on other players or groups are prohibited
  • Vehicle attacks with intent to kill/incapacitate, especially at high speeds, are not allowed
  • Multiple characters from the same player in one gang/group or roleplay story is forbidden
  • Realistically portray fear when threatened with weapons or severe harm
  • Spontaneous shootouts without reason are not permitted
  • Antagonizing gangs, police, or armed individuals without valid character motivation is prohibited

Powergaming and Fail RP

Powergaming is defined as using in-game mechanics, external information, or roleplay concepts to gain an unfair or unrealistic advantage. Examples include:

  • Exploiting game mechanics for rapid money-making instead of roleplaying
  • Using "/me" chat to force RP or gain advantages
  • Government employees engaging in corruption or law-breaking without Management approval
  • Using keybinds (e.g., "/e sleep") during combat for unfair advantages
  • Misuse of /propfix or /refreshskin commands

Fail Roleplay (RP) occurs when a player acts unrealistically, intentionally causes disruption, or engages in trolling. Examples include:

  • Escorting people while in a vehicle instead of using the "Put in vehicle" feature
  • Seating hostages on bikes or motorcycles
  • Driving or ramming at unrealistic speeds without legitimate character motivation
  • Giving radio callouts while incapacitated
  • Using shared emotes (e.g., "piggyback") to exceed vehicle capacity
  • Failing to properly roleplay injuries
  • Relaying information while incapacitated

New Life Rules

  • Characters remain alive in all situations except when going DOA (permanent character death)
  • Always attempt to contact EMS/PD for medical assistance when incapacitated
  • After EMS examination, memory of previous events should be determined by injury severity and type
  • After respawning without EMS treatment, you must forget all events leading to your character's death
  • "Kyle Rayner cannot take a LOA"
  • After revival, do not involve yourself in the same scenario again. Avoid all associated individuals for at least 20 minutes
  • Revenge RP is permitted but may face administrative consequences if it significantly impacts the community
  • Revenge tickets will not be tolerated. Accept losses in conflicts or wars

Combat Logging

Combat Logging is defined as exiting the game to avoid roleplay situations, including but not limited to:

  • Avoiding robbery and loss of items
  • Evading arrest and processing by law enforcement
  • Exiting the server after the timer expires but before respawning

Report all crashes or disconnects immediately in our Discord server. Upload crash log zip files to the designated Discord channel.

Criminal Rules

  • Criminal activities are limited to groups of 5 players, including phone group limitations
  • All members of a phone group must be actively engaged in the same scenario
  • Hostages may not be exchanged for property, items, or money
  • Consistently prioritizing combat-oriented players or "grinders" over roleplayers is prohibited

Class Information

Note: These are baseline guidelines. Each situation will be handled according to its specific circumstances. Consequences may vary based on severity and past infractions:

  • Class A - Warning or up to a 1-day ban
  • Class B - Warning or 1-3 day ban
  • Class C - 7-day ban or permanent ban
  • Players may appeal permanent bans after 30 days

Perilous RP reserves the right to update and modify these rules at any time. All rule changes will be announced in the Perilous RP Discord. Punishments are at the discretion of Perilous RP staff and ownership.

Gang Rules

General Rules

Toxic behavior in the Gang Discord is strictly prohibited and will result in immediate removal, with the possibility of a server ban depending on severity. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Sexism
  • Racism
  • Any form of discrimination or harassment

High-ranking gang/MC members are prohibited from having high-ranking LEO, EMS, or DOJ alternate characters. High-ranking roles include:

  • GANG high ranks:
    • Boss
    • Underboss
    • Captain
  • MC high ranks:
    • President
    • Vice President
    • SGT at Arms
  • LEO high ranks:
    • SASP
      • Colonel, Lt. Colonel, Major, Captain
    • LSPD
      • Chief, Asst. Chief, Deputy Chief, Captain
    • BCSO
      • Sheriff, Under Sheriff, Major, Captain
  • DOJ includes ALL DOJ positions
  • SAFR High Ranks
    • Chief
    • Assistant Chief
    • Deputy Chief
    • Captain
  • HOSPITAL high ranks:
    • Medical Director
    • Chief of Medicine
    • Assistant Chief
    • Chief Admin
  • Gang Staff participation:
    • Gang Staff may be part of gangs but CANNOT hold high-ranking positions within a gang (as listed above)
  • Prohibited behaviors include, but are not limited to:
    • Trash talking after being incapacitated or failing to roleplay injuries
    • Verbally abusing defeated opponents
    • Disrespectful actions towards incapacitated players
    • Excessive force after incapacitating an opponent (mag dumping). Exception: Minimal force to facilitate robbery. Staff can discern between necessary action and excessive violence
  • Discord calls while in-game are strictly prohibited, as they violate Perilous' third-party communication rule. Violation risks immediate suspension of your gang's whitelist status or extended wait times for whitelist applications.
  • Revenge tickets are NOT TOLERATED. Accept losses in conflicts or wars.
    • All tickets regarding conflicts require footage from AT LEAST 2 minutes before and after the reported incident/rule violation.
    • Submitting petty, salt-induced, or revenge reports may result in Warning Points (WPs).
  • Business ownership limits for Gangs/Families/MCs:
    • Allowed to OWN or CO-OWN up to 2 businesses
    • Examples:
      • Two members each owning 2 businesses
      • Two members co-owning a single business
      • One member owning a business and another co-owning a separate business
  • Incorporating gang activities within your business may subject you to LEO searches if probable cause is established.
  • Law Enforcement presence at gang conflict scenes is permitted. PD may have specific policies for these situations, but their presence does not constitute a rule violation.

Establishing Whitelist

  • All organization establishments must be initiated through staff.
  • Upon gang staff approval, you may begin representing your gang (colors, turf claims, etc.).
  • The organization leader must submit a gang ticket confirming NAME, ROSTER, and LETTER OF INTENT.
  • Gang staff will review the information within 72 hours.
  • A minimum of 5 full members (excluding associates or hang-arounds) is required before applying for official gang status.
  • Maximum organization size: 20 members.
  • Upon staff approval, a 2-week trial period commences to demonstrate RP quality.
  • During the trial, prepare MLO, EUP, colors, and sprays.
  • On the final day of the trial, the gang leader should contact staff for approval status. If accepted, gang staff will forward MLO, EUP, etc., to Dev Staff for implementation.
  • If denied official whitelisted status, a 7-day waiting period is required before reapplying with Gang Staff.

Gang Rules and Regulations

  • Solo contracting: Permitted under these conditions:
    • Solo contractor must be an existing gang member
    • Group size limits:
      • 20 individuals (for turf defense)
      • 4 individuals (for offensive operations)
  • Solo contractor payment: Fixed at $50,000 per person.
  • Financial agreements must be documented via video or signed screenshot.
  • Contract evidence must be submitted through a gang ticket in the main Discord.
  • Contracts are valid for a single scene only.
  • Prepayment for multiple scenes is prohibited.
  • Solo contractors must adhere to hiring gang's colors and clothing requirements.
  • DISCLAIMER: Utilizing a solo contractor may affect gang reputation if the contractor switches allegiances.

Gang Designated Medics

  • Each gang may designate 1-2 members as medics during conflicts.
  • Gang medics must remain neutral and cannot participate in combat activities.
  • Medic vehicles must be painted orange, and medics must wear only orange clothing.
  • Medics may attempt to retrieve incapacitated members before LEO arrival, provided SAFR is not already on scene.
  • Comply with LEO instructions to avoid violating NVL rules.
  • Orange-clad medics should not be targeted by any gang.

Striking System

  • Rule violations by 3+ gang members result in a strike against the gang.
  • Accumulation of 3 strikes will trigger staff intervention and appropriate disciplinary action.
  • For non-whitelisted gangs:
    • Strikes are recorded and may impact future whitelist approval.
    • 3 strikes extend whitelist approval waiting period by 30-60 days, at staff discretion.
  • Strike appeals can be initiated by the gang leader through a gang ticket.
  • Strike removal is at the discretion of gang staff/high management.

Turf

  • Whitelisted gangs are allocated a "compound" area.
  • All gangs may claim an area as their "turf".
  • Home turf guidelines:
    • Extends up to a 3-block radius around the compound.
    • May include additional areas where benches are located.
  • Whitelisted groups:
    • May claim a maximum of TWO (2) benches.
    • Can control up to a 2-block radius around these locations.
  • Public/Community benches:
    • May be subject to taxation when in use by others in your presence.
    • Not valid grounds for turf wars unless the conflict is solely over the turf itself.
  • Gang colors must be worn when confronting other gangs.
  • Turf acquisition rules:
    • Challenging gangs have a 72-hour window to claim contested turf.
    • Failed attempts after 72 hours must be abandoned.
    • Successful claims may be countered by the displaced gang through declaring war or accepting the loss.
  • ALL GANG CONFLICTS REQUIRE PROPER INITIATION (except during declared gang wars).
  • Long-term turf control may be subject to staff intervention to prevent monopolization.
  • Gaining control of another gang's turf does not grant access to their MLO. Original owners retain MLO access.

Business Rules

Business Bureau Staff

Business Bureau Representatives are responsible for ensuring business compliance with established regulations. Their duties include overseeing tax collection, addressing complaints, and collaborating with server developers and management to maintain a stable economy.

Business Bureau Discord

All Perilous Roleplay businesses will be included in a central Business Discord. This platform facilitates:

  • Rapid dissemination of announcements from the Business Bureau to all owners/employees
  • Dedicated sections for each business to enable internal communication

While separate, unofficial Discord servers are permitted, the Business Bureau reserves the right to intervene if rule violations or toxic behavior are reported. In such cases, businesses may be required to add a Bureau staff member to ensure a respectful environment.

Businesses are encouraged to host or sponsor city-wide events. To promote your event or request additional resources, please notify the Business Bureau through the appropriate channels.

Owner Expectations

Business ownership entails significant responsibilities. Upon acquisition, owners will be assigned a Business Bureau representative as their primary point of contact. Owners are expected to:

  • Actively maintain and grow their business
  • Dedicate substantial time to business operations
  • Maintain transparent communication with the Bureau

Prospective owners unable to meet these expectations should refrain from applying.

Business Ownership Limits

  • Players are limited to owning one business initially
  • After four OOC months of successful operation, owners may apply for a franchise (second business of the same type)
  • Franchise applications require a comprehensive proposal and continued active management of the original business
  • Franchise owners may appoint a co-owner but remain responsible for both establishments

Business Sales

Business sales protocol:

  1. Owner must notify their Bureau representative of intent to sell
  2. Owner must propose two suitable candidates to the Bureau
  3. If candidates are unsuitable, the Bureau will open applications to other players
  4. Former owner may provide input on new applications
  5. Proof of transaction must be provided to the Bureau

The Bureau can facilitate sales, but this forfeits the owner's input and incurs a commission. After the first transfer, businesses can only be sold back to the Bureau, which will then auction them to other players.

Owner Regulations

  • Business owners are prohibited from holding upper management positions in other businesses
  • Neglect (failure to maintain stocks, equipment, or proper employee management) may result in ownership transfer
  • Extended leaves of absence (over one month) may lead to business seizure
  • Ownership reinstatement after extended absence is subject to Bureau evaluation

Criminal Affiliations and Businesses

  • Gang members may own businesses but must not directly affiliate them with criminal organizations
  • Gang-related activities must be kept separate from business premises
  • Violation may result in law enforcement intervention and possible business seizure
  • Business owners/employees may defend their property against attacks but are encouraged to contact law enforcement
  • Roleplay takes precedence in conflict scenarios

Trespassing

  • Businesses retain the right to refuse service for justifiable reasons
  • Permanent trespass orders require proper legal channels through law enforcement and the DOJ
  • Repeated gang violence should be reported to gang staff
  • If a trespassed individual must enter the only open business in the city, they may do so briefly for essential services

For further clarification on trespassing issues, consult law enforcement.

About the San Andreas State Police

The San Andreas State Police (SASP) is a premier law enforcement agency dedicated to maintaining public safety and upholding the law across the state of San Andreas. Established to serve and protect the diverse communities within the state, the SASP is committed to professionalism, integrity, and excellence in all its operations.

With a team of highly trained officers, the SASP provides a wide range of services, including traffic enforcement, criminal investigations, emergency response, and community outreach. Our mission is to ensure that every resident and visitor of San Andreas feels secure and confident in the safety of their environment.

We believe in fostering strong partnerships with local communities, other law enforcement agencies, and public service organizations to enhance the quality of life for all. Through rigorous training, advanced technology, and a commitment to justice, the San Andreas State Police strive to be a model of effective and ethical law enforcement.

About the San Andreas Fire and Rescue

San Andreas Fire and Rescue (SAFR) is a dedicated emergency services organization serving the state of San Andreas. SAFR is committed to protecting life, property, and the environment through comprehensive fire suppression, rescue operations, and emergency medical services. With a team of highly trained firefighters, paramedics, and support personnel, SAFR responds to a wide range of emergencies, including structural fires, vehicle accidents, medical emergencies, hazardous material incidents, and natural disasters.

Operating out of multiple fire stations strategically located throughout San Andreas, SAFR is equipped with state-of-the-art fire engines, ambulances, rescue vehicles, and specialized equipment to handle any situation. SAFR members undergo rigorous training to ensure they are prepared for all scenarios, from firefighting and technical rescues to advanced life support and disaster response.

SAFR is committed to community safety, continuously engaging in public education, fire prevention initiatives, and community outreach programs to reduce the risk of emergencies and enhance public awareness. Through collaboration with other emergency services and agencies, SAFR strives to maintain the highest standards of service, professionalism, and excellence in protecting the residents and visitors of San Andreas.
